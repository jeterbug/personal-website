How I work

Build it right. Then make it better.

01

Map the system before touching it

Before changing anything, I understand how it actually works — not how it's supposed to. That means talking to the people in the process, finding where time disappears, and identifying what's genuinely broken versus what just feels that way.

02

Design for iteration, not perfection

A good process today beats a perfect process never. I build things to be tested, adjusted, and improved — with feedback loops built in from the start so the system gets smarter over time without a major overhaul every quarter.

03

Automate the mundane. Protect the creative.

Repetitive work isn't just inefficient — it crowds out the thinking that actually moves things forward. I look for every task that can be systematized or eliminated, so the people doing the work can spend their energy on what only they can do.
